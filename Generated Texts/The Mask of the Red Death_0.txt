

The Mask of the Red Death is a short story by Edgar Allan Poe. It was first published in Graham's Magazine in May 1842. The story is set during a time of pestilence and tells of a prince who tries to avoid the plague by hiding away in a secure abbey. He decides to hold a masquerade ball, but things take a turn for the macabre when a mysterious guest wearing a mask resembling a corpse appears and begins dancing. The prince is seized with fear and ultimately dies. The story is an allegory for the inevitability of death.
