

1. French and Spanish are both Romance languages that are part of the Indo-European language family. 
2. Both languages use Latin-based alphabets and share similar pronunciation and written conventions. 
3. French and Spanish both have subject-verb-object word orders in their sentences and both use many cognates.
4. Both languages retain many features of Latin grammar, including gender and verb conjugations, though they evolved differently. 
5. French and Spanish cultures have a lot in common, both of which have influenced each other in the form of art, literature, music, and cuisine.
